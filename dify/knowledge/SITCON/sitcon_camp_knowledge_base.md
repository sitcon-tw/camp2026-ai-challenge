## 1. What is SITCON?

**SITCON** stands for **Students' Information Technology Conference** (學生計算機年會).

SITCON is a student-driven community in Taiwan (and the broader East Asia region) dedicated to:
- Promoting **information technology (IT) education** among students
- Spreading the **open source spirit**
- Providing students a platform to **share and present** technical knowledge and projects

SITCON is described as the **largest student IT community in East Asia**. It organizes students at all levels — from junior high school through graduate students — and is run entirely by student volunteers.

Key characteristics:
- **Organized by students**, for students
- Features **student speakers**
- Covers **technical talks, open source advocacy, research projects, and student-relevant issues**
- Has inspired the founding of **SITCON x HK** (Hong Kong) and the **Student Open Source Conference** in Chongqing, China
- Provides support to student groups and IT communities worldwide

## 2. What is SITCON Camp?

**SITCON Camp** (SITCON 夏令營) is an annual **summer camp** run by the SITCON community. It is a 5-day, 4-night residential IT camp primarily aimed at **high school students** and early college students in Taiwan who want to get into the field of information technology.

The camp blends **structured learning** with **hands-on practice** and **community interaction**. It is not a typical school class — it combines coding courses, a hackathon, community challenges, a vision café, and open-source community activities all within a five-day immersive experience.

The camp has been held **every July** since 2014 (with some exceptions), consistently at **National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University (NYCU), Guangfu Campus**, Hsinchu, Taiwan.

As of 2026, SITCON Camp has reached its **10th edition**.

## 3. Origin and Founding

### SITCON's Birth (2012)

SITCON was founded in **August 2012** by **Denny Huang (黃一晉)**, a student who attended **COSCUP** (Conference for Open Source Coders, Users and Promoters), Asia's largest open source conference. Inspired by COSCUP, he asked: *"Why don't we create a conference where students can stand on a stage and share their own projects?"*

He quickly rallied students online, named the event **SITCON (Students' Information Technology Conference)**, and was elected as the inaugural General Coordinator (總召).

SITCON's founding was supported by **37 schools** across high schools and universities in Taiwan, co-organized with:
- **OSSF** (Free Software Forge at Academia Sinica's Research Center for Information Technology Innovation — 自由軟體鑄造場)
- **ITSA** (Ministry of Education's IT Talent Cultivation Program — 教育部資訊軟體人才培育計畫)

### First Annual Conference (2013)

The first SITCON Annual Conference was held in 2013.

### SITCON Camp Begins (2014)

In **2014**, SITCON expanded its activities to include a **summer camp** under the theme of open source learning. The first SITCON Camp (called "SITCON Summer Camp 2014") was themed **"Open Source Community, Innovative Learning for Tech People"** (開源社群，創新學習科技人). It cost NTD 5,000 per person and introduced the format that would continue for the next decade.

## 4. Mission and Values

SITCON Camp's core beliefs:

- **Laying the groundwork for IT education** at a student level
- Providing a **friendly, accessible entry point** for students interested in computer science
- **Sowing the seeds of open source** culture
- Encouraging students to not just consume technology but **contribute to it**
- Promoting **self-directed learning** and community participation
- Supporting **educational equity** — financial aid is available for students from lower-income backgrounds or remote areas

SITCON welcomes participants of **diverse identities and backgrounds**, and actively encourages participation from **women, sexual minorities, and underrepresented groups**.

## 7. Camp Activities and Format

### Core Format

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Duration | 5 days, 4 nights |
| Typical Timing | July (annual) |
| Venue | NYCU Guangfu Campus, Hsinchu, Taiwan |
| Participants | ~54–60 students per year |
| Staff | Student volunteers (team leaders, instructors, TAs) |
| Language | Primarily Mandarin Chinese |
| Accommodation | On-campus dormitories |
| Meals | All provided during the camp |

### Activity Types

#### 1. Main Curriculum (主線課程)
The backbone of the camp. Instructors guide participants from scratch through a specific technology stack. In earlier years this focused on Python + Telegram Bot development; in 2025 it was Python + FastAPI + AI-assisted front-end; in 2026 the curriculum split into three specialized tracks: Software Engineering, Cybersecurity, and AI.

Teaching assistants (TAs) and instructors circulate during sessions to provide one-on-one help, ensuring no one gets left behind.

#### 2. Hackathon (黑客松)
A multi-day hackathon embedded within the camp. Teams form, ideate, prototype, and present a project by the final day. Participants go from concept discussion → project scoping → development → demo/presentation, simulating a real engineering sprint.

"Hackathon" = "Hacker" + "Marathon" — a marathon of programming and engineering where a team builds something distinctive in limited time.

#### 3. Elective Tracks / Breadth Courses (廣度課程)
Guest lecturers from industry and academia present topics beyond the main curriculum — past topics include Artificial Intelligence, IoT, cybersecurity, CSS, generative AI, and more. These "breadth courses" are designed to broaden participants' vision of what the IT field encompasses.

#### 4. Vision Café (視界咖啡館)
Inspired by the World Café format. Prominent seniors from the IT field participate in small-group, conversation-style discussions with camp participants. This breaks down the typical speaker–audience hierarchy and enables direct, personalized interaction and mentorship.

#### 5. Open Source Spirit Talk (開源精神短講)
Seniors active in open source communities share their experiences and encourage participants to engage with open source projects and communities. Associated with a **Community Expo (社群博覽會)** where IT-related communities set up booths with games, demos, and introductions.

#### 6. Challenge Activities / Checkpoint Game (闖關活動)
A programming and logic puzzle game integrated into the camp. Participants use the skills they've learned to solve puzzles in a team setting, reinforcing the connection between coding and real-world problem-solving.

#### 7. Vision Café (視界咖啡館)
A World Café-style session with senior figures from tech and adjacent fields. Designed for close, interactive conversation rather than formal talks.

- Python (basic syntax, data structures, functions, package management)

**Specialized Tracks (2026 onwards):**
- Software Engineering (architecture, engineering practices, AI-assisted coding)
- Cybersecurity (real-world attack vectors, DEF CON-style thinking)

**Community & Open Source:**
- Open source spirit and community culture
- Community challenge activities

## 11. Community Ecosystem

SITCON is more than just a camp. It operates a broader ecosystem:

### Events Run by SITCON
1. **SITCON Annual Conference (年會)** — Held every March; multi-track IT conference with student speakers; the flagship event

3. **SITCON Meetup (定期聚)** — Regular regional meetups connecting IT students across Taiwan
4. **SITCON Hackathon (黑客松)** — Standalone hackathon event (scaled up from HackGen events in 2014–2015)

## 13. Registration and Logistics

### Typical Registration Process
1. Registration period opens in spring (April–May)
2. Applications are reviewed (not first-come-first-served)
3. Acceptance list published approximately 3 weeks before camp
4. Accepted students complete payment to finalize enrollment
5. **Financial aid** is available for students with financial need
6. **Group discount** applies for 2–4 person groups registering together
7. Early bird pricing available for registrations before a set deadline
