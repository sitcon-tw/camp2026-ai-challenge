## 3. Background & 10th Anniversary Context

SITCON Camp was first held in **2014**, making 2026 its **10th edition** — a milestone anniversary. The camp has accompanied countless students with a passion for technology over the years, evolving as the broader tech landscape has changed.

**Why the camp was created (original mission):**
> As computer science becomes an increasingly essential discipline in the tech era, the SITCON team believes that laying the groundwork for IT education, providing a friendly entry point for students interested in computer science, and sowing the seeds of open source culture is a very important and worthwhile endeavor.

## 4. This Year's Direction: The Big Shift

SITCON Camp 2026 marks a **deliberate repositioning** of the camp's mission and format — the most significant change in the camp's 10-year history.

### The Problem Being Addressed

As online learning resources (tutorials, documentation, courses) have proliferated, basic programming knowledge has become much more accessible. There is now **a large group of students** who have already crossed the entry threshold — they may have learned Python in class, written a few small programs — but find themselves lost when confronted with the wider landscape: cybersecurity, competitive programming, AI, systems programming, open source contributions, etc.

**The camp's diagnosis:** These students don't need another beginner Python tutorial. They need **orientation, context, and a guide** into what the different sub-fields of IT actually look like in practice.

### The Repositioning

| Previous Positioning | 2026 Repositioning |
|---|---|
| Beginner-oriented (入門導向) | Exploratory & Guided (探索型 / 導覽型) |
| Single curriculum for all | Three specialized tracks |
| Focus on a language/tool | Focus on a field/mindset |
| Entry-level Python → Bot | SE / AI / Cybersecurity deep dives |

**Official statement from the camp:**
> To respond to the proliferation of learning resources and the changing era, SITCON Camp has decided to break away from the traditional beginner-entry framework, repositioning the camp as an "exploratory" and "guided" IT journey. Beyond continuing the classic community challenge, Vision Café, and hackathon activities, we hope to transform the open source spirit into guiding starlight, accompanying students as they discover their genuine specializations and passions in the vast field of information technology.

### The Camp's 2026 Approach
- **Invite domain experts** from each field to teach — people with real, impressive credentials
- **Start from accessible entry points**, but go deep into core concepts quickly
- **High-intensity hands-on practice** to give students a genuine taste of each field
- Help students **identify which sub-field they want to invest in long-term**

## 5. Three Core Tracks

### 5.1 Software Engineering Track

**Instructor:** Denny Huang (黃一晉)

**Instructor Background:**
- Co-founder of SITCON (Student IT Conference)
- 2025 IT Matter Awards — Open Source Community Contribution Award winner
- Google Developer Community core organizer
- Former data analytics team manager at a major gaming company

**Track Title:** "Reject Fragile Vibe Code: Those who only use AI to write code are destined to be the first wave of casualties in the tech industry"

**Core Pitch:**
> Using AI to write code is nothing to be proud of. "Vibe Code" patched together by intuition is often a digital disaster that cannot withstand scrutiny. Being able to make a program run is only the bare minimum; understanding how to plan architecture is the irreplaceable value. This course will let students experience real refinement: watching a chaotic project collapse, then using industry-grade "software engineering" thinking to bring it back under control — transforming students from mere "code cobblors" into "software architects" with a macro perspective.

**What Students Will Learn:**
- A project is not just code that runs — it must be **understandable, collaboratable, and maintainable**, and must continue to function when requirements change
- Software Engineering thinking: architecture, design, testing, collaboration practices
- Why requirements clarification, architecture design, testing, and maintenance become **more** important when AI can write more of your code

> The barrier to network attacks has fallen to an all-time low — innovation without cybersecurity protection is just prey waiting for hackers. This course will lead students to switch into the reverse-thinking mode of a "world-class hacker," dissecting real attack risks. Students will learn to find fatal flaws in seemingly perfect systems. **What Students Will Learn:**
- Cybersecurity thinking: understanding how **attackers and defenders view the same problem differently**

## 7. Activity Descriptions (Full Detail)

The following are the official descriptions for each activity at SITCON Camp 2026, sourced directly from the schedule page.

---

### Opening Ceremony (開幕)
> From the opening ceremony, participants enter the five days and four nights of SITCON Camp. Students will understand the camp's core theme, activity rules, and the upcoming course arrangement, and prepare to begin learning and hands-on practice together with their team members.

---

### Pioneer/Introductory Course (先導課程)
> Before officially entering the main curriculum, this session leads students to complete the setup of the computer classroom environment, common tools, and development platform configurations, ensuring subsequent hands-on work can proceed smoothly. At the same time, the course will guide students to understand basic concepts of development environments, so that even after leaving Camp, students can replicate the setup process on their own computers and continue with independent learning and project development.

---

### Elective/Breadth Course (廣度課程)
> Content to be announced (TBA at time of data collection). Broadens students' perspective of the IT field beyond the three main tracks.

---

### Challenge Activities (闖關活動)
> When AI has already become part of the world, how should those living within it understand and use technology? The challenge content, designed around three major directions — software engineering, machine learning, and information security — will combine elements of observation, analysis, and teamwork, allowing everyone to gradually understand the thinking methods and application scenarios behind IT through interaction and exploration. Perhaps the answers exist not only in code, but also in the process of team collaboration and each act of reasoning. Are you ready to step into this tech exploration together?

---

### "Let Your Agent Fight!" (讓你的 agent 上場戰鬥吧)
> In a digital camp attack-and-defense game, students will challenge themselves to design their own exclusive agent through writing code. From defensive formations to attack paths, every decision will be automatically executed by the code the students design. In this hands-on activity that tests logical thinking and strategic planning, students will experience how algorithms actually operate, observe different problem-solving approaches, and iterate toward optimal strategies through trial — defeating all comers.

---

### Software Engineering Main Course (軟工主線課程)
> The core of Software Engineering Day is helping students understand that a project is not just about making code run — it must be **understandable, collaboratable, and maintainable**, and must continue to move forward when requirements change.

---

### AI Main Course (人工智慧主線課程)
> The AI and Machine Learning themed course will lead students to understand that a model is not magic, but a tool closely related to **data quality, problem definition, and validation methods**.

---

### AI Coding Experience Exchange (AI 寫程式經驗交流)
> More and more people now use AI to assist with programming — from generating prototypes and understanding error messages to modifying code and adding tests. But the more convenient the tools, the more likely new problems arise: Is the code it wrote actually correct? Would modifications break existing functionality? How do we judge when we can trust it?
>
> In this post-course exchange session, students can share their experiences using AI coding tools with classmates — discussing what methods they've tried, what problems they've encountered, and what approaches have actually been helpful. After a full day of Software Engineering courses, we will also look back together and think: when AI can help us write more code, why do requirements clarification, architecture design, testing, and maintenance become even more important?

---

### "Breaking the Urban Legends of Bit City" (破解位元城的都市傳說)
> In the age of advanced AI, communication between people has become sparse. Beyond chatting with AI, some people even ask AI to analyze how to reply to others' messages. But no matter how advanced artificial intelligence becomes, human exchange remains indispensable. In the leisure time after courses, let's get our bodies moving and — together with new friends from the camp — explore the famous story of the "Heisenberg Bug"!

*(Note: "Heisenberg Bug" refers to a computing bug that changes behavior when it is observed or debugged — a humorous reference to Heisenberg's uncertainty principle.)*

---

### Cybersecurity Main Course (資安主線課程)
> Cybersecurity Day will bring students close to the thinking methods of the cybersecurity field. Cybersecurity is not just about finding vulnerabilities or solving puzzles — it is about understanding how systems operate, and how **attackers and defenders view the same problem**.

---

### Escape Room (實境解謎)
> The escape room will hide clues in various corners of the camp venue. Students need to leave the classroom, observe the environment, and search for hints and key information scattered around. Each puzzle is not just a simple Q&A — it requires combining observation, reasoning, discussion, and team division of labor to gradually approach the answer.
>
> In this activity, students will explore the venue with their team members, exchange ideas, verify guesses, and may find new angles from others' perspectives when stuck. Through the puzzle-solving process, everyone gets to familiarize themselves with the camp space in another way outside of courses, and accumulates experience of completing tasks together through collaboration and reasoning.

---

### Open Source Spirit Talk (開源理念分享)
> "Why do we organize SITCON? Probably so that we have someone to go for late-night snacks with." — Rifur, 2013
>
> For many newcomers, IT communities are incredible places: everyone seems to speak their mind, be bold in expressing themselves, support each other, and rarely worry about seniority or hierarchy. In this brief talk, I will try to use simple language to cover the history of IT community culture development, introduce how community members collaborate with each other, and help everyone understand where to find learning resources — or even how to make meaningful contributions to projects you like.

---

### Community Expo (社群博覽會)
> The IT world is vast and boundless. "Learning alone without friends leads to narrow-mindedness." So let's explore different communities! Beyond SITCON, there are many different IT communities with various focuses, but all sharing the same love for IT. We have selected several communities — from open culture to Python to cybersecurity — where you can meet a group of "friends" to make your IT exploration journey even more exciting.

---

### Vision Café (視界咖啡館)
> The Vision Café is inspired by the World Café format. In this summer camp, we have invited prominent seniors from various fields and communities, transforming the traditional panel discussion format into a conversational style. Students can interact closely with famous figures in the IT world. By shortening the distance between speakers and students, speakers can give more targeted advice to students, and students are encouraged to actively ask questions and participate, thereby generating productive two-way communication.

---

### Fireside Chat (爐邊夜談) — *New in 2026*
> Not enough from the daytime courses? Pull up a chair, have some late-night snacks, and let's keep talking.
>
> Inheriting the spirit of the well-received "Vision Café," this year's Fireside Chat brings even deeper exchanges. As the aroma of hot late-night snacks fills the air, staff members from various fields will become "booth hosts" at each table.
>
> Want to know how the alignment techniques behind large language models work? Curious about what pitfalls first-line industry developers face? Or want to hear a cybersecurity expert share attack-and-defense secrets? We'll lift the veil on the world of information technology.
>
> Here, there is no instructor on stage and no students below — just a group of people passionate about IT. Move around freely, sit wherever you like. The most authentic technical knowledge and inspiration will be born in late-night casual conversation over snacks.

---

### Closing Ceremony (閉幕)
> The closing ceremony will bring the five days' story together, turn the gaze toward life after camp, and connect this experience to the larger IT community.

## 16. SITCON Ecosystem Overview

SITCON Camp 2026 exists within a broader set of activities organized by the SITCON community, each targeting a different stage of a student's IT journey:

| Activity | Chinese | Purpose | Stage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hour of Code** | 程式入門課 | One-hour introductions to CS; lower the entry barrier | Complete beginner |
| **SITCON Camp** | 夏令營 | 5-day immersive camp; turn curiosity into real skills; find direction | Entry → Exploration |
| **SITCON Annual Conference** | 年會 | Multi-track tech conference; stage for student speakers | Advanced sharing |
| **SITCON Hackathon** | 黑客松 | Standalone hackathon; turn ideas into real products | Applied building |

**Community philosophy:**
> SITCON starts from students. Through the annual conference, Hour of Code, Camp, and Hackathon, it brings IT education, technical exchange, and open source collaboration to different stages of learning. We hope that everyone interested in IT can find an entry point, meet companions, and see the direction they can move forward through hands-on work and sharing.

**Media coverage:** SITCON and the Open Culture Foundation were featured on **Epoch MUCH's "Taiwan's New Pride" (台灣新驕點)** program, highlighting how SITCON and student communities drive IT exchange, open source culture, and next-generation talent development.
